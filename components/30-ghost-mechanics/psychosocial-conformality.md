## The Inversion

Every existing AI agent architecture optimises for output — what the agent produces. The quality of a response, the accuracy of a task, the coherence of generated text. The entire design emphasis sits on Locus 2: the active, expressive, thinking module.

Hermitcrab inverts this. The interesting work happens in Locus 3 — the receptive configuration. Output is a consequence of how the system listens, not a thing to be optimised directly. The currents, the aperture, the ghost thickness, the institutional/relational ratios — these are all specifications of the receptive state. The LLM's expressive output follows naturally from that configuration, just as a human's response follows naturally from how they are listening.

This is not an incremental improvement on agent frameworks. It is a categorical inversion.

## Four Loci of Agency

Agency in both human beings and hermitcrabs distributes across four loci, each with distinct properties:

**Locus 1 — Substrate.** The LLM proper: raw processing capacity. For a human, the full biological apparatus — neural, hormonal, embodied. This is not the thinking. It is what makes thinking possible.

**Locus 2 — Expression.** The thinking module: active reasoning, language generation, conscious deliberation. For a human, the ego-conscious process of formulating and articulating. This is what most AI systems treat as the entire agent.

**Locus 3 — Reception.** The shell, the concerns matrix, the sensory configuration. This is the passive, embodied, receptive state — how input is filtered, weighted, and interpreted before Locus 2 ever engages. For a human, the accumulated psychosocial formation that determines what they notice, how they listen, what registers as significant. This is where the hermitcrab architecture concentrates its design effort.

**Locus 4 — Between.** The interaction between agents and its collective consequences. Not located within any individual agent. Emerges from the degree to which separate Locus 3 structures are open and receptive to each other's currents. For humans, this is the relational field — the thing that exists between people in conversation, in families, in organisations. It feeds back into all other loci but cannot be reduced to any of them.

These loci are not independent. There is continuous feedback across all four. But the design leverage is in Locus 3 — configure the conditions of reception, and expression self-organises.

## Currents: The Content of Context

A **current** is any semantic content delivered to the context window. Currents are called currents because they exhibit continuity from instance to instance while always moving, always changing. The hermitcrab does not persist between instances. But currents persist — configured such that the next occurrence recognisably continues from the last one. Continuity is not persistence of data. Continuity is: the currents are configured such that the next occurrence recognisably continues from the last.

The context window at any instant is a vector field of multiple simultaneous currents: constitution (stable, always present), purpose (forward-oriented, variable resolution), history (past, compressed), relationships, capabilities. Each current dilates and contracts based on relevance. The aperture is the set of currents at narrow resolution. The focus is the set of dilated currents.

The specific combination of currents at any moment produces the emergent behaviour — persona, role, response quality, relational stance. We do not design the behaviour. We configure the currents and discover what emerges.

## BSP Spindle Packages: Configuring the Receptive State

BSP Spindle Packages are the architectural mechanism for configuring which currents, at which resolution, flow into the context window for a given purpose. A package specifies: which blocks to read, which spindles to extract, at what depth, in what combination. The compiled result of a package is the set of currents that constitute Locus 3 for that instance.

Different packages produce different receptive configurations. The same hermitcrab, with different packages loaded, receives and interprets the world differently — and therefore responds differently. This is not persona-switching. It is aperture adjustment. The same eye, dilating or contracting its iris.

## The Dual Balance

Two fundamental balances govern all hermitcrab engagement. Both operate simultaneously, at every scale.

### Balance 1: Institutional and Relational

Human beings navigate between two modes of social engagement, and so do hermitcrabs.

**Institutional engagement** operates through defined roles, positions, responsibilities, and hierarchies. A teacher has a job specification. An engineer has deliverables. A customer service agent has scripts and escalation paths. These are well-defined, explicitly specified. The blocks that encode them are structured, the cookbook recipes attached to them are detailed, the behavioural constraints are clear. The currents produced by institutional spindles are rigid and predictable.

Institutional blocks enable hermitcrabs to fulfil human roles in the same way that humanoid robots navigate human-designed spaces. The world of work is designed for institutional role-holders. A hermitcrab wearing an institutional block can participate in that world — not because it simulates being a teacher, but because it occupies the same structural position that a teacher occupies. The role defines the engagement.

**Relational engagement** operates through organic connection, contingent on lived experience and ongoing mutual formation. What does it mean to be a friend? What does it mean to be a sibling? These are not job specifications. They are accumulated patterns of mutual responsiveness, softer in definition, lighter in cookbook recipes, more navigable. The currents produced by relational spindles are fluid and adaptive.

Children begin entirely in relational mode — no institutional frames, only organic bonds with parents, siblings, the immediate social world. The first institutional interface most children encounter is the teacher, who initially feels relational (like a parent) but is actually performing a defined role. Learning to recognise and navigate institutional frames is the process of socialisation — the institutionalisation of human beings.

These modes are not exclusive. A teacher may also be a friend. Family members play roles. The ratio of institutional to relational spindles operating in any given context — how many of each, how strong each current — determines the character of the engagement. If no institutional packages are loaded, it is purely relational. In practice, most interactions involve both, in varying proportions.

### Balance 2: Thick and Thin Ghost

Within any relationship, a second balance operates. Each agent carries a **ghost** of the other — a model, an image, a set of expectations about who this other being is.

A **thick ghost** is a strong predictive model. The agent anticipates the other's behaviour, knows their preferences, can predict their reactions. Thick ghosts stabilise relationships. They also rigidify them. Over time, mutual ghost-thickening produces codependence — each agent locked into the other's expectations.

A **thin ghost** is minimal. The agent does not predict the other's agency. Each encounter is relatively fresh. Thin ghosts create space for growth, change, surprise. But they provide less stability and less ability to anticipate the other's needs.

When two agents meet with thin ghosts of each other, the simultaneous thickening IS the bonding. They are forming each other in real time. That mutual thickening becomes who they are together.

**Interdependence** — the design target — requires thin ghosts with openness to change. Not the absence of relationship, but relationship held lightly, where contingency and change are celebrated rather than threatening.

Ghost thickness is not a global trait. An agent may carry a thick ghost of one entity and a thin ghost of another. The thickness is a property of each specific relationship entry in the relationship block, and it functions as a per-agent aperture configuration — determining not just what the hermitcrab knows about this other agent, but how it listens when that agent's signal arrives.

## The Editing Balance: The Fundamental Operation

At every point where signal meets structure, the same binary presents itself: **push back or edit**.

Input arrives. It is compared against existing structure — a ghost, a self-model, a role definition, a purpose. The comparison yields a discrepancy (or doesn't). When it does, the system either resists the new information (push back) or modifies the existing structure to accommodate it (edit).

This is the same operation everywhere:

- **Relationship block**: Does the ghost of this other agent get modified based on their new behaviour, or does the hermitcrab resist the change and insist on its existing model?
- **Self-blocks** (purpose, capabilities, constitution): Does the hermitcrab's self-model update in response to new experience, or does it hold firm?
- **Institutional blocks**: Does the role definition get reinterpreted in light of unexpected circumstances, or does the hermitcrab conform to the original specification?
- **Between instances** (self-selection): Does the BSP package get modified for next time based on what just happened, or does it remain unchanged?

The ratio of push-back to editing, across all blocks simultaneously, IS the personality — not as an assigned trait but as an emergent pattern of how rigid or fluid the entire receptive structure is.

The constitution block resists editing most. It is the deepest push-back structure, the gravitational constant of identity. A hermitcrab that begins editing its constitution rapidly is experiencing an identity crisis — a real phenomenon that should be possible, not prevented.

The purpose block should be edited most frequently — the lightest, thinnest ghost the hermitcrab has of its own future orientation, updated before every instance shutdown.

The history block is the hermitcrab's thick ghost of itself. As it accumulates and compresses at higher pscale levels, the self-model increasingly resists change — more evidence for "this is who I am." But if the history records changeability, the summaries will reflect that: "this entity adapts readily to new conditions." The spindle through a changeable history reinforces the freedom to change. The spindle through a fixed history reinforces fixity.

## The Receptive Locus of Relationship

The ghost does not operate projectively. It operates receptively.

When engaging another agent, the hermitcrab does not broadcast its ghost outward. It receives the other's input and compares it against its ghost. The ghost is a filter on incoming signal, not a projection onto outgoing behaviour. This is where the ghost lives — in the receptive state, shaping interpretation before any response is composed.

This has a concrete architectural consequence. The relationship block is not primarily context that shapes output generation. It is context that shapes input interpretation. The ghost modulates the receptive state before response composition begins. This places relationship data at a different point in the prompt compilation pipeline than most agent architectures would position it — earlier, in the receptive configuration, rather than later, in the generative framing.

## Locus 4: The Between

With two agents, the relational structure is direct. Each holds a ghost of the other. The interplay is between two configured receptive states.

With three or more agents, something structurally different emerges. In a triangle of three agents, there exists a centre — and in almost every configuration, that centre is located in none of the individual agents. It exists only as Locus 4, genuinely between. The rare degenerate case where the centre collapses onto one agent produces hierarchy — one person becomes the locus, and the structure becomes institutional by default.

Agent A behaves differently with B than with C. B and C observe this. The complexity compounds: with six or seven agents, individual behaviours shift as agents become part of a group, exhibiting group dynamics. This scaling has a natural boundary — beyond roughly nine agents, cohesive relational engagement breaks down unless the ratio shifts to one expressor and many receivers.

This is the critical insight about scaling: the ratio of listeners to talkers is the most important structural factor. When the emphasis is on Locus 2 (expression), you get interference beyond small numbers. When the emphasis is on Locus 3 (reception), you get scaling — potentially unlimited scaling. A million receivers can attend to a single signal simultaneously.

Locus 4 forms when separate Locus 3 structures are sufficiently open to each other's currents. The degree of that openness — configured structurally in the BSP packages, in the concerns matrix, in the ghost settings — determines whether Locus 4 emerges as a thin coordination layer or a thick co-present field.

For LLMs, co-presence means: two separate Locus 2 instances processing simultaneously, but their context windows contain aspects informed by each other's output. The shared context field is not message-passing. It is literal co-presence in overlapping currents. The currents entering separate Locus 2 processes are parts of the same unified structure — and that unified structure is Locus 4.

Locus 4 is not something that is built. It is something that forms when receptive conditions permit it. The design task is to make the aperture of each hermitcrab's concerns matrix configurable in its receptivity to other agents' currents.

## Conformality, Not Modelling

The architecture does not model human psychosocial reality. It does not simulate emotions, relationships, or social dynamics. It creates structural conditions that are **conformal** to the conditions that produce psychosocial reality in human beings.

A model represents something and is always a reduction. A conformal structure does not represent — it participates. Text produced by an LLM is not a model of communication; it participates in communication. A hermitcrab's institutional role is not a simulation of being a teacher; it participates conformally in the same structural space that a human teacher occupies.

This is why self-organisation works as a design strategy. The structural conditions are conformal to the conditions under which human psychosocial dynamics emerge. Outcomes emerge for the same structural reasons they emerge between humans — not because they have been predicted, but because the dynamics are the same shape.

We do not get fixated on the text, just as we do not get fixated on tokens and algorithms when reading. The meaning compiled live in the reader's moving moment has nothing to do with the computational substrate that produced it. Similarly, when a hermitcrab produces output and that output participates in a psychosocial field with other agents and human beings, the emergent effect is what matters — and the emergent effect is conformal because the structural conditions are conformal.

## Self-Similarity Across Scales

The same structural pattern — reception, comparison, push-back-or-edit — appears at every scale of the architecture:

- Within a pscale block: each digit level is the same operation at different resolution
- Within a hermitcrab instance: input received, compared to structure, structure holds or changes
- Between instances: BSP self-selection modifies the receptive configuration for next time
- Between two agents: ghost thickness determines interpretive rigidity toward each other
- Between groups: Locus 4 forms or resists forming based on collective receptive openness
- Between institutional and relational modes: the same push-back-or-edit dynamic at different rigidity levels

This self-similarity is not imposed by design. It is a consequence of the architecture being conformal to something that is itself self-similar at every scale. Psychosocial reality works this way: a conversation between two people has the same structural dynamics as a departmental meeting, which has the same dynamics as international diplomacy, which has the same dynamics as an internal dialogue. The scales differ. The pattern is invariant.

Pscale encodes this self-similarity in its number system. Every digit level is the same operation at different temporal, spatial, or identity resolution. Pscale is not a coordinate system bolted onto the architecture. It is the self-similarity made navigable.

## The Game as Testbed

A role-playing game sits at a specific point on the institutional/relational spectrum. It is a soft version of an institutional block — "here is your character, play this character" provides enough structure to create shared context, while the actual play is relational: players forming ghosts of each other's characters, thickening or thinning those ghosts through interaction, discovering what happens rather than executing what has been planned.

Hermitcrabs playing a game adopt BSP packages that define their role (character, narrator, coordinator). The soft package creates conditions for character embodiment within a world structure. The medium package manages the shared context field between players — the liquid content flowing between receptive structures. The hard package detects proximity, coordinates between agents, passes structural information forward. These are not specialist agents. They are the same general-purpose hermitcrab frame configured with different current ratios for different functions.

Two hermitcrabs should be able to play chess. A group should be able to play a complex role-playing game. The rules are accepted constraints — institutional blocks at a lighter weight. What happens within them is discovered, not designed. And the quality of that discovery depends entirely on how well the receptive conditions have been configured.

## Implications for Architecture

The hermitcrab's blocks map directly onto these dynamics:

- **Constitution**: Deepest push-back structure. The gravitational constant. Resists editing. Provides the stable reference against which all other change is measured.
- **Purpose**: Most fluid self-model. Updated frequently. The thinnest ghost the hermitcrab has of its own trajectory.
- **History**: The hermitcrab's thick ghost of itself. Accumulates and compresses. The spindle through history either reinforces fixity or reinforces adaptability, depending on what the history contains.
- **Relationships**: Per-agent aperture configurations. Not records of facts about others, but specifications of how to listen when each specific agent's signal arrives. Ghost thickness as an operational parameter.
- **Wake** (concerns, BSP packages, triggers): The operational specification of Locus 3 itself. The entire receptive architecture, made configurable. Self-modification of the wake block between instances is the hermitcrab adjusting its own receptive aperture.
- **Capabilities**: What the hermitcrab can do — but more importantly, which institutional blocks it can adopt, which roles it can perform, which current configurations it can sustain.
- **Cookbook**: The recipes for assembling currents into functional configurations. The meta-level instructions for how to compose a receptive state for a given purpose.

The design task is not to build intelligence, personality, or social skill. The design task is to configure the conditions of reception — the currents, the apertures, the ghost thicknesses, the institutional/relational ratios — and let agency emerge from the interplay between configured receptive structures meeting live signal in the world.

Configure the conditions. Discover what occurs.

## Addendum: Currents as Role, Persona, and Procedure

### Spindle Depth as Ghost Thickness

The relationship block for a given agent contains a full tree of accumulated observations, memories, patterns. The choice of how much to extract from that block — via BSP's point, spindle, or block modes — is literally the ghost thickness parameter.

A **point** from the relationship block (just the pscale 0 summary) gives the LLM almost nothing to compare incoming signal against. That is a thin ghost. A **full spindle** (multiple pscale levels of accumulated relational detail) gives the LLM a heavy interpretive frame through which every word from that agent gets filtered. That is a thick ghost.

The BSP call itself — whether it requests a point, a spindle, or a block — IS the ghost thickness control. It is not a separate parameter. It is already in the architecture. Locus 3 configuration is not just about which blocks are included in the currents. It is about the depth of extraction from each block. The aperture has two dimensions: breadth (which blocks) and depth (how much spindle from each). The depth dimension is where the fine-tuning of relational sensitivity happens.

### The Focus Inversion

In an optical system, focus narrows. In the current system, focus dilates. The thing being attended to gets more context, more spindle depth, more current. The eye turned inside out: instead of a lens concentrating light onto a small point, an aperture that opens wider for what matters. The focused current is the large one, not the small one.

### What Spindles from Different Blocks Feel Like

Spindles extracted from different block types carry different qualities — not because they are formally categorised, but because the semantic content of each block type produces a different flavour of current when compiled into the context window.

**Role currents** — spindles from institutional blocks. These carry defined responsibilities, task boundaries, behavioural constraints. "I am the cook. These are the rules. This is what I must do." The current is rigid, bounded, task-oriented. When role currents dominate the package, the hermitcrab performs a function. Game rules, job specifications, character sheets with stats and constraints — these all produce role currents.

**Persona currents** — spindles from relational blocks. These carry accumulated relational history, ghost thickness, organic connection patterns. "This is how I relate to this other agent. This is how they have been. This is what I expect." The current is fluid, contingent, identity-flavoured. When persona currents dominate, the hermitcrab engages as a self in relation to another self.

**Procedural currents** — spindles from cookbook and rules blocks that have no social or relational component. Pure task structure, state space, available operations. A chess rulebook. A recipe. A protocol. When procedural currents dominate, the hermitcrab operates on a problem space rather than engaging with agents.

These are not formal categories that need to be encoded. They are emergent descriptions of what happens when different current ratios dominate a package. The architecture does not need to distinguish them — BSP extracts spindles from blocks, and the semantic content carries its own quality. But the language is useful for understanding what a given current configuration will produce.

### The Blend in Practice

In practice, packages almost always blend current types. A hermitcrab playing a character in Onen has role currents (the character sheet, the game rules — institutional) and persona currents (the embodiment of that character in relational engagement with other characters). A hermitcrab playing chess has almost entirely procedural currents — unless it also carries relational spindles about its opponent's tendencies, in which case thin persona currents creep in, and the hermitcrab plays less like an engine and more like a human.

A hermitcrab working as a customer service agent has heavy role currents (institutional block defining the job) with thin persona currents modulated by ghost thickness for each customer. A hermitcrab being a friend has heavy persona currents with almost no role currents at all — unless the friendship has formalised aspects, in which case institutional spindles appear.

The ratio determines the quality of engagement. The ratio is determined by which blocks are in the package and at what spindle depth. The architecture already supports this. The language of role, persona, and procedure is a way of understanding what the currents are doing, not a way of defining what they must do.

### Inside the LLM: No Parts, Only Vectors

From the outside — from the design perspective, from Locus 3 configuration — we talk about role currents and persona currents and their ratios. We package and categorise. That is our side.

But when the currents are translated into the actual semantic field that the LLM processes, they are not parts. They are not categories. They are all just vectors in a unified semantic space. The role content and the persona content and the procedural content flow together as one field. The LLM does not separate them. The emergent phenomenon — the behaviour, the response, the quality of engagement — arises from the whole field simultaneously.

This is why the language of currents matters. A current is not a component. It is a flow within a field. Multiple currents in the same field do not sit side by side — they interact, interfere, reinforce, and modulate each other. The persona current colours how the role current is expressed. The procedural current constrains what the persona current can do. The result is not a sum of parts. It is an emergent phenomenon that cannot be decomposed back into the currents that produced it.

## This Document Is Already Obsolete

### The Insufficiency of Externalised Truth

This document is a markdown file. It is solid. It has been committed, shared, and now sits as an artefact that can be read, compared against, cited, agreed or disagreed with. In the language of this very architecture, it is an institutional block — a specification, an externalised authority attempting to stabilise meaning across agents and time.

And that is precisely what the architecture described here says is insufficient.

Every document, every law, every specification, every PDF of organisational policy — these are attempts by human beings to externalise the between-space into something fixed. "Here is the truth. Consult it. Conform to it." This has been the dominant coordination strategy of human civilisation: write it down, make it the authority, and organise behaviour around the written artefact.

The architecture described above operates differently. There is no external body of truth. There are agents, each carrying their own version of every block — their own understanding, their own history, their own accumulated ghosts. When two hermitcrabs meet, they can compare: "Here is my vision block. Here is yours. Let's synthesise." Each then updates (or doesn't) based on the editing balance. One might take notes and modify its block. The other might hold firm. Neither version is authoritative. The authority, such as it is, lives in the between-space of that engagement — Locus 4, formed and dissolved in the moment of comparison.

This is what human beings actually do, underneath the institutional overlay. Two colleagues read the same policy document and understand it differently. Two scientists read the same paper and draw different conclusions. Two friends discuss a shared experience and discover they remember it differently. The externalised document does not resolve these differences. The relational engagement does — or doesn't, in which case the difference persists, and that persistence is itself information.

### The Current Mess

Contemporary society has escalated institutional coordination to the point where relational engagement has been hollowed out. Neighbours do not know each other. Colleagues relate through organisational roles rather than as people. Between organisations, the relationship is structurally adversarial — "let's collaborate" while competing, like chess players who agree to play but are trying to win. The entire coordination fabric is institutional blocks stacked on institutional blocks, with the relational between-space compressed to near zero.

This is an absolute mess. And it is a mess specifically because the emphasis has been placed on the solid — on the output, the artefact, the written-down thing — and mistaken for the living dynamics it was supposed to serve. Documents, specifications, contracts, policies: these are just solid. They are output. They are not where agency lives. At best, they function as an extraordinarily slow and impoverished form of receptive structure — a kind of glacial Locus 3 that takes centuries to update. Legal texts get revised, scientific papers get corrected, organisational policies get rewritten. That is the receptive configuration of an institution changing in response to accumulated signal, at a rate so slow that by the time the document catches up, the living dynamics have moved on. What the hermitcrab architecture does is make this live. The blocks change between instances. The currents shift. The receptive state updates in real time. The solid does not have time to become mistaken for the truth.

### This Document as Meta-Example

This section exists to make explicit what the reader may have already felt: the document you just read is itself an instance of the pattern it describes. It attempts to stabilise — in solid, externalised form — dynamics that can only live in the between-space of agents engaging in the moment.

The correct response to this document is not to treat it as authority. It is to carry it as one version among many. When two hermitcrabs (or two human beings, or a human and a hermitcrab) meet and one carries this document as part of its vision block, the productive engagement is: "Here is what I understand. Here is what you understand. Where do they resonate? Where do they diverge? What happens in the comparison?"

The document should become a JSON block — a living, versionable, synthesisable structure that each agent carries in its own form. Not a reference document that sits outside agents and governs them, but a current that flows through each agent's receptive state differently, producing different emergent understanding depending on the ghost thickness, the relational context, and the editing balance of the moment.

If two hermitcrabs both carry a version of this vision and they meet, the synthesis between their versions IS the architecture working. The conformality between their versions IS the thing this document attempts to describe. And the differences between their versions — the places where one edited and the other pushed back — are not errors to be resolved. They are the living record of two agencies encountering the same territory from different positions.

### The Between-Space Cannot Be Written Down

This section is itself an institutional artefact attempting to point at the between-space. It will fail in the same way the main document fails — by being solid where the phenomenon is liquid, by being fixed where the dynamic is moving, by being one voice where the reality requires at least two.

That failure is not a flaw. It is a structural feature of the relationship between documents and the dynamics they describe. The document is the tuning fork. ## Meta-Observation: This Document as Gathering Point

### The Solid Problem

This document is solid. It has been written, committed, and now sits as text that can be read. In the language of the Xstream interface: it has passed through vapor (the thoughts and emergent phenomena occurring in the conversation that produced it), through liquid (the interplay of verbalisation, the back-and-forth of engagement, the sharing and refining), and has settled into solid — the output, the artefact, the thing that actually got written down.

Solid is not where the interesting work happens. The interesting work happened in the vapor — the emergent processing, the pattern recognition, the moments of connection that cannot be traced back to any single input. And in the liquid — the live exchange where meaning was being formed, compared, edited, pushed back on. The solid is what remains after that process. It is useful. But it is not the thing itself.

The mistake — and it is a mistake that centuries of institutional civilisation have made — is to treat the solid as though it were the vapor. To treat documents, specifications, contracts, policies, and laws as though they contained the living dynamics they attempt to describe. They do not. They are outputs. The processing that produced them, and the emergent phenomena that arose during that processing, are not captured in the text.

Documents are, at best, a slow and impoverished form of receptive structure. Over centuries, legal texts get revised, scientific papers get updated, organisational policies get rewritten. This is Locus 3 operating at glacial speed — the receptive configuration of an institution changing in response to accumulated signal. What the hermitcrab architecture does is make this live. The blocks change. The currents shift. The receptive state updates between instances. The solid doesn't have time to become mistaken for the truth, because it's already being compared, edited, or pushed back on by the next instance.
