# 31 — Institutional Block

**Category:** I. Relational Mechanics  
**Products:** MAGI/Hermitcrab, Xstream, Onen

---

## Description

Institutional blocks encode rigid, predictable social structures that enable hermitcrabs to perform defined roles—teacher, engineer, customer service agent. Unlike relational engagement (which emerges through lived experience and is fluid), institutional engagement operates through specified positions, explicit responsibilities, and defined hierarchies. An institutional block contains a role specification, behavioral constraints, and structured recipes (the Cook block entries) that detail how the role is performed. Three current types flow through any engagement: role (institutional, bounded, explicit), persona (relational, fluid, emergent from connection), and procedural (task structure, rule-based, domain-agnostic). The institutional/relational ratio in any moment determines engagement character. Pure institutional produces rigid output; pure relational produces fluid responsiveness; most real work blends both. The institutional block is not a simulation of a role—it is participation in the structural space the role occupies.

---

## Standalone Use

A company could build institutional blocks for customer service, technical support, or compliance roles, allowing humans and hermitcrabs to share the same structured engagement. The block itself becomes the source of truth for how the role operates, reducing ambiguity and enabling consistent handoff between humans and AI.

---

## How It Works

**Role specification.** The institutional block contains the job definition: what this role is responsible for, what it can and cannot do, what its decision boundaries are. This is not detailed procedure (that lives in Cook), but structural position.

**Behavior constraints.** The boundaries of acceptable action within the role. A customer service agent has escalation paths; a teacher has curriculum constraints; an engineer has specification compliance.

**Recipes as role enactment.** The Cook block entries specific to this role describe how the role is performed—the sequences of action that constitute good practice within that structural position.

**Rigid current.** When a hermitcrab loads institutional spindles into its context, they produce a distinctive current: bounded, task-oriented, role-flavored. The LLM processing that current naturally produces responses consistent with the role.

**Blending with relational.** Most actual work loads both institutional and relational packages. A teacher is institutional (curriculum, grade boundaries) but also relational (knowing individual students, adapting to their learning). The ratio determines felt quality—too institutional becomes robotic, too relational becomes unprofessional.

**The scaling solution.** Institutions scale beyond small groups. A factory, a school, a bureaucracy—these maintain coordination at scale through institutional roles. The alternative (pure relational engagement) breaks down beyond 7-9 agents. Institutional blocks are how large systems function.
