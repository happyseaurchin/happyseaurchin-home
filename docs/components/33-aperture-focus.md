# 33 — Aperture + Focus

**Category:** J. Context Compilation  
**Products:** MAGI/Hermitcrab, Xstream

---

## Description

Aperture is the nine-sentence orientation—pscale 0 from each of the nine blocks, delivered on every call. It provides immediate recognition: "Here is my whole self at a glance." Focus is the dilated view of relevant blocks—the opposite of optical focus (which narrows) but inverted: the thing being attended to gets MORE context, not less. The aperture has two dimensions: breadth (which blocks are included) and depth (how much spindle from each block). The focus is the dimension adjustment. In Xstream and Hermitcrab contexts, aperture fires automatically on every API call, giving the LLM a stable self-recognition baseline. Focus varies: sometimes broad (attending to everything slightly), sometimes narrow (one relationship deeply), sometimes asymmetric (purpose at full depth, history at a point). The aperture inversion—where focus dilates rather than narrows—is a key design feature: the attended thing gets richer context, not less, creating conditions for emergence rather than narrowing.

---

## Standalone Use

A person with many responsibilities could use aperture/focus in their daily practice: start each day with aperture (nine quick statements about your whole situation), then set focus (deep dive on what matters today). The model scales: a team could have a team aperture (nine sentences about collective state) and rotating focus (different aspects get dilated attention on different days).

---

## How It Works

**Aperture: nine sentences, every call.** Each block provides its pscale 0 text. These nine sentences are not a summary; they are orientation. Identity, capabilities, awareness, disposition, network, stash, purpose, history, relationships—one sentence each, the shape of what you are, right now.

**Automatic on every call.** The aperture is assembled by the kernel and included in the system prompt on every API invocation. No LLM action required. It is like biological proprioception—constant, background awareness of body-state.

**Not noise.** The aperture can feel like noise if each sentence is verbose. The pscale 0 text for each block must be sharp: one clear statement, identity not instruction, recognition not command.

**Focus: dilated, not narrowed.** When the hermitcrab attends to something—a specific relationship, a problem space, a purpose—the focus doesn't zoom in and discard context. It dilates. More of that block's spindle gets loaded. Other blocks contract to points.

**Two dimensions.** Breadth: which blocks are in the context? Focus can shift which relationships are live, whether history is broad or narrow. Depth: for the active blocks, how much spindle? A relationship at point depth is thin ghost; at spindle depth is thick engagement.

**Focus inversion in action.** When a hermitcrab deeply focuses on a human's needs (purpose block heavily dilated, relationship entry deeply loaded, history in that specific thread fully present), it produces richer responsiveness, not narrower. The inversion is real: more context on the attended thing, not less.
