# 10 — Concept-to-Pscale

**Category:** Creation Layer  
**Products:** MAGI/Hermitcrab, Xstream

---

## Description

Concept-to-Pscale organizes ideas into structured territory using a different logic than temporal flow. While Transcript-to-Pscale captures the *journey* of thought over time, Concept-to-Pscale captures the *landscape* of ideas in logical relationship. A concept block arranges ideas hierarchically by generality, dependency, or functional relationship rather than when they were spoken or written. When paired with a transcript block, the two enable diagnostic comparison: the journey (how a thinker moved through ideas) versus the landscape (how those ideas actually relate to each other). This pairing reveals whether the speaker's path was efficient, where they doubled back, and where their mental model might be incomplete or contradictory.

---

## Standalone Use

A designer or strategist could use Concept-to-Pscale to map a domain before implementation — organizing requirements, constraints, and interdependencies into a pscale structure. The block becomes a navigable blueprint: broad abstractions near the root, concrete specifications deeper down. Unlike a mind map (which is visual but not navigable), a pscale concept block is both: you can walk it with the spindle operator to see context at any depth level.

---

## How It Works

**Domain segmentation**: The system begins with a scope definition (e.g., "authentication system", "meeting facilitation protocol") and identifies major conceptual branches.

**Hierarchical decomposition**: Each branch is expanded vertically by logical dependency. More abstract concepts sit higher in the tree; more concrete implementations sit deeper.

**Relationship annotation**: Cross-branch dependencies are noted (using star references) to capture how concepts in one area affect another. These annotations are NOT the primary navigation path but become visible when walking spindles across boundaries.

**Generality ordering**: Unlike temporal ordering, concepts are ordered by how fundamental they are. A general principle appears before its special cases.

**Completeness checking**: The pscale block reveals gaps (branches that terminate early, suggesting unexamined areas) and redundancies (similar concepts appearing in separate branches, suggesting either unification or specialization).

**Landscape vs. journey**: When compared against a transcript block from the same domain, the concept block highlights whether the speaker explored the landscape systematically or jumped around. The divergence itself is diagnostic.
