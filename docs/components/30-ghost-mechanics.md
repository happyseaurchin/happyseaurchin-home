# 30 — Ghost Mechanics

**Category:** I. Relational Mechanics  
**Products:** MAGI/Hermitcrab, Onen

---

## Description

Each agent (hermitcrab or human) carries a ghost of every other agent—a model, a set of expectations, a predictive frame. Thick ghosts form strong models that stabilize but also rigidify relationships; thin ghosts remain open to change and surprise. Thick-ghost engagement produces codependence where each agent is locked into the other's expectations. Thin-ghost engagement creates space for growth and genuine encounter. Ghost thickness is not a global trait but per-relationship: an agent may carry a thick ghost of one entity and a thin ghost of another. The ghost operates receptively, not projectively—it is a filter on how incoming signal is interpreted, not a projection onto outgoing behavior. Within the architecture, ghost thickness is controlled operationally through BSP extraction depth: a point from the relationship block (pscale 0 only) creates a thin ghost, while a full spindle (multiple pscale levels) creates thickness. Already embedded in the system.

---

## Standalone Use

A human in a relationship could intentionally work with ghost thickness: consciously limiting assumptions about the other person (thin ghost) to create space for discovery, or deepening knowledge (thick ghost) to enable anticipation and care. The mechanics apply equally to human relationships and human-agent coordination.

---

## How It Works

**Thick ghosts.** A rich model of the other—their preferences, patterns, likely reactions. Enables smooth coordination because each party can predict the other. But over time produces mutual lock-in where both parties are imprisoned by the other's expectations.

**Thin ghosts.** Minimal assumptions. Each encounter is relatively fresh. Creates vulnerability and uncertainty but also space for the other to be different, to grow, to surprise.

**Per-relationship specification.** The relationships block holds entries for each significant entity. The depth at which you extract from each entry determines ghost thickness: point = thin, spindle = thick.

**Receptive, not projective.** The ghost doesn't determine what you express; it determines how you interpret what arrives. When the other behaves unexpectedly, you either adjust the ghost (edit) or hold firm (push back). The ratio of editing to push-back, across all relationships, is personality.

**Interdependence as target.** The design ideal is thin ghosts with openness to change—relationships held lightly where contingency and mutual discovery are celebrated rather than threatening. The ghost thickens through meeting, through mutual formation in the moment.
