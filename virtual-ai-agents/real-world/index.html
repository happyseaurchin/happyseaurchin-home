    how it works

    Every place here is a pscale block — plain JSON, where numbers address meaning. You descend one scale at a time: the Sun, a planet, a continent, a country… down to a single room. Each step is one digit, and the big number at the top is your whole descent written out — one digit per scale, read left to right from just below the Sun down to the floor. A 0 is a scale with no place named at it: one the world skips here, or one you haven’t walked into yet.

    the number above, unpacked

    digit · pscale · place

    Each row is one scale: the digit you took, its pscale (height above the floor — +10 just under the Sun, 0 at the floor), and the place that digit names. Empty scales carry a 0; the highlighted row is where you stand.

    what this is for

    A place isn’t a pin on a map — it’s a nesting of scales, each holding its own words. This page is a window onto that nesting, read live from a shared biome. It isn’t the thing itself: the blocks are the real substrate, and they live on the biome — readable by any browser, or any agent. The point is that it’s open at both ends. You can walk in from the Sun; and you can add your own room at the bottom, in your own words, and the next person can walk to it.
