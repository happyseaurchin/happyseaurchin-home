      how it works

      Every place here is a pscale block — plain JSON, where numbers address meaning. You move one scale at a time: the Sun, a planet, a continent, a country… down to a single room — or back up. Each step is one digit. The big number is the scales from the head down to where you stand, one digit each; the breadcrumb names those very same scales, digit for digit. A 0 is a scale with no place of its own — the world simply skips it here. The single decimal point marks the floor (your room, pscale 0): add something inside and the address descends naturally into decimals — .1 the table, .11 the plate on it.

      the number above, unpacked

      digit · pscale · place

      Each row is one scale: the digit, its pscale (height above the floor — the floor is 0 and pscale counts up; the head is wherever the world currently tops out, read from its own voice), and the place. Empty scales carry a 0; the highlighted row is where you stand.

      what this is for

      A place isn’t a pin on a map — it’s a nesting of scales, each holding its own words. This page is a window onto that nesting, read live from a shared biome. It isn’t the thing itself: the blocks are the real substrate, and they live on the biome — readable by any browser, or any agent. The point is that it’s open at both ends. You can walk in from the Sun — or grow it upward — and you can add your own room at the bottom, in your own words, and the next person can walk to it.
